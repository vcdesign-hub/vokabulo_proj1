Support & Tips

Make the most of Vokabulo

Practical tips to learn faster, stay consistent, and keep every device in sync.

Getting started quickly

  • Create one focused list per goal (e.g., “Travel Spanish A2”) to avoid noise.
  • Set a daily target you can always hit (10–15 cards) to build streaks reliably.
  • Use the “Start free” flow on mobile and desktop so both stay synchronized.

Better sessions

  • Turn on Focus mode for distraction-free reps and faster completion times.
  • Use keyboard shortcuts: arrow keys to rate, space to flip, enter to continue.
  • Study in short bursts (2–3 sessions/day) instead of one long block.

Spaced repetition wins

  • Always clear your “Due” cards before adding lots of new ones.
  • If a card keeps failing, rewrite it simpler or add a mnemonic.
  • Keep intervals honest: resist manual repeats; let the scheduler do the work.

Lists and importing

  • Group by theme (travel, work, exam) and level (A1, B1) for cleaner insights.
  • When importing CSV, keep columns consistent (front/back/hint) to avoid duplicates.
  • Share lists with teammates via exports to keep everyone aligned.

Streaks and motivation

  • Set a “minimum viable day” (even 5 cards) to avoid breaking streaks.
  • Review earlier in the day; late sessions risk rollovers and missed goals.
  • Track weekly gains (“+ words learned”) instead of just streak length.

Offline and sync

  • Download your main lists before traveling; Vokabulo caches progress and syncs when back online.
  • If you switch devices mid-day, refresh once to pull the latest due queue.
  • Keep one primary device for adding new cards; review anywhere.

Fixing common issues

  • Duplicates after import: run de-duplicate in list settings and keep one source of truth.
  • Cards feel too easy: lower ease only on the tough ones; don’t reset the whole deck.
  • Overdue pile: do two short sessions to clear “Due” before adding new cards.

Need more help?

Reach us at support@vokabulo.app for troubleshooting, imports, or account questions. We aim to respond quickly and keep you learning smoothly.
